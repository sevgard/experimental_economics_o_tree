Instructions

In this study, you will play in a formed group of {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants. Each participant in the group is given {{ Constants.endowment }}. The group has the opportunity to undertake a joint project. Each participant in the group decides how much she or he is going to invest in the project.

The game is played once and has 20 rounds: The initial investment of all players in your group is set to zero. In every round the investment increases by 10 points and you (and other players) have to make a decision whether you want to invest additional 10 points in the joint project or exit the game. Your final level of investment in the joint project is determined by your investment level displayed on the page at the moment you exit the game. If you exit the game, you cannot make investment any more decisions.

The earnings from the project are calculated as follows: investments of all {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants in your group are added up and the total investment is multiplied by a factor of {{ Constants.multiplier }}. The resulting amount is the individual share, which is equal for all {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants.

Income from the project = individual share = sum of investments in the project * {{ Constants.multiplier }}

Your payoff equals your earnings from the project plus the amount you did not invest in the joint project.

Earnings = ({{ Constants.endowment }} - investments in project) + {{ Constants.multiplier }} * sum of all investments in the project

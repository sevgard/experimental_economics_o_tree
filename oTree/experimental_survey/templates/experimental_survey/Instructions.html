All participants will be divided in groups of four members. Except us, the experimenters, nobody knows who is in which group. Unless you reveal how much you earned to the other participants of the experiment, nobody except for us and you will get to know how much you earned.

The decision situation

You will be a member of a group of 4 people. Each member has to decide on the allocation of 200 points (your endowment). You can put these 200 points on a private account or you can invest them fully or partially in a project. Each point you do not invest in the project will be automatically transferred to your private account. You make multiple decisions on your investment, but only one of them will be relevant for your earnings. Which investment decision is going to be payoff-relevant is described below. The game in the first stage is conducted only once.

Your income from the private account

For each point that you put on your private account, you will earn exactly one point. For example, if you put 200 points on your private account (which implies that you do not invest anything in the project) you will earn exactly 200 points. If you put 60 points into the private account, you will receive an income of 60 points from the private account. Nobody except you earns something from your private account.

Your income from the project

From the number of points you invest in the project each group member will get the same payoff, the so-called individual share. For each group member the income from the project, so the individual share, will be determined as follows:

Income from the project = individual share = sum of all investments in the project * 0.5

For example, if the sum of all investments in the project is 200 points, then you and all group members get a payoff of 200 x 0.5 = 100 points from the project.

Total income

Your total earnings are the sum of income from your private account and the project:

Earnings = (200 - investment in project) + 0.5 * sum of all investments in the project

Investment decision
In the experiment, each subject has to make two types of decisions. In the following we will call them "unconditional" and "conditional" investment.
Unconditional investment: Here you decide on the investment in the project, regardless of how much the other players in your group invest.
Conditional investment: Here you make an investment decision depending on how much the other players in your group invested on average. You will see 21 fields that ask you how much you would invest in the project if the remaining three group members invested on average "x" points (rounded). "x" starts at "0 points" and increases by "10 points" until "200 points".

You first make the unconditional investment decision and afterwards the conditional investment decisions. You are assigned to one of two roles:
Unconditional role: For three out of the four group members, the unconditional investment is taken for computing the earnings of the first stage of the experiment.
Conditional role: For the remaining fourth group member, one conditional investment decision is taken for computing the earnings of the first stage of the experiment. For this, the average unconditional investment is computed for the three "unconditional" players. This average is rounded to the nearest multiple of 10 and then matched with the respective conditional investment of the "conditional" player.
When you make your unconditional investment and conditional investment decisions, you of course do not know know whether you will be selected by the random mechanism as the conditional player. You will therefore have to think very carefully about both types of decisions because both can become relevant for you.

You will be provided with an on-screen calculator and pen and paper.

Example: Suppose you were chosen to be the conditional player. The other three team members' unconditional investments are 60, 70, and 120. The average investment therefore is 250/3=83. This is rounded to the nearest multiple of 10, so 80. If you indicated in your conditional investment decisions that you would contribute 100 points if the rest of the team invested on average 80 points, your investment to the project is 80 points. Your earnings therefore are (200-80)+0.5*(60+70+120+100)=295 points.
